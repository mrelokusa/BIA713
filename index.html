The zero departure rate at 7+ years is also worth unpacking. This is likely a survivorship effect — employees who make it past 6 years have already self-selected as committed to the company. They may also benefit from accumulated seniority, better compensation, or informal status that makes leaving less attractive. It does not necessarily mean the company suddenly improves; rather, the employees most likely to leave have already done so by that point.
